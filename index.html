RATAN TATA

28.12.1978-09.10.2024

Biography:

Ratan Tata (1937-2024) was an Indian industrialist and philanthropist who served as the chairman of Tata Group and Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012, and as interim chairman from 2016 to 2017. He was known for his leadership in transforming the Tata Group into a global conglomerate and for his significant contributions to social causes.

Early Life and Education:

  • Born in Mumbai, India, in 1937.
  • Son of Naval Tata and Sooni Commissariat.
  • Received formal education at various schools, including Campion School, Mumbai, Cathedral and John Connon School, Mumbai, and Riverdale Country School in New York City.
  • Graduated from Cornell University with a degree in architecture and later attended Harvard Business School for an advanced management program.

Awards and Recognition:

  • Padma Vibhushan (India's second-highest civilian award)
  • Padma Bhushan (India's third-highest civilian award)

The Tata Nano was not just a car—it was a symbol of Ratan Tata's belief in improving the quality of life for the common Indian, empowering families to own a four-wheeler that would otherwise be out of reach

TATA NANO

Ratan Tata's vision for the Nano was a car that would be affordable for the Indian masses, offering a safe and reliable alternative to two-wheelers. He was inspired by seeing families precariously riding motorcycles, especially children sandwiched between parents. The Nano aimed to provide a more comfortable and secure mode of transportation for these families.
